Platelet Rich Plasma Therapy

HOW DOES THE VAMPIRE FACELIFT DIFFER FROM ANTI WRINKLE INJECTIONS?

Anti Wrinkle Injections actually works by relaxing the muscle. This procedure (Vampire Facelift) is to restore volume to the skin.

IS EVERYONE A GOOD CANDIDATE FOR A VAMPIRE FACELIFT?

Those with extreme loss of flexibility in the skin are not a good candidate. Automatically don’t rule yourself out. Young adults and middle-aged people see better results. For others, ClinicGen.Next’s Dermatology clinic offers an array of anti-aging treatments like anti wrinkle injections, fillers, and thread lift. 

WILL THERE BE ANY INCISIONS MADE TO MY SKIN?

The Vampire Facelift is a simple, nonsurgical, non-invasive procedure that involves micro needling followed by infusion of platelet rich Plasma onto the skin.

IS THERE ANY DOWNTIME ASSOCIATED WITH THE PROCEDURE WHERE I WON'T BE ABLE TO ENGAGE IN EVERYDAY ACTIVITIES?

Micro needling causes a little redness which may last a day. 

ARE THERE ANY SIDE EFFECTS ASSOCIATED WITH A VAMPIRE FACELIFT?

Even when the PRP a is done with micro needling, the epidermis remains intact and which is why almost all common side effects of laser resurfacing etc. Can be neglected.

HOW LONG WILL IT TAKE TO SEE RESULTS?

Results are quick. You will start seeing the results as soon as the redness subsides.

HOW LONG WILL THE RESULTS LAST?

The clinical studies have shown it can last from one to two years, which is pretty impressive for a nonsurgical procedure.

WHAT RESULTS SHOULD I EXPECT?

You can expect the added Volume to the face in areas where there has been a muscle and collagen decrease, improved skin tone, shape, and texture. It also diminishes the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles.

HOW MANY SESSION DOES ONE NEED?

The protocol is one session once a month for 3 months followed by a maintenance session once a year. 

WHY IS THE PROCEDURE CALLED A VAMPIRE FACELIFT?

This is because the procedure does deal with your blood. In the Clinic ,  we refer to the procedure as PRP, which is the medical reference to the procedure, which stands for Platelet Rich Plasma.
